First Mow — Operation Ground Zero
  • 01Walk the entire yard. Remove rocks, sticks, dog toys — anything the mower blade turns into a weapon.
  • 02Set mower deck to the HIGHEST setting. 4 to 4.5 inches. All the way up.
  • 03Attach the bag. You are not mulching this. You are collecting evidence of what was living here.

SEC-04
WEED INTEL
// Know your enemy · Pre-emergent prevents · Post-emergent eliminates
📡Pre-emergent = the bouncer. Stops weed seeds at the door before they germinate. Post-emergent = the guy who throws out the ones who already snuck in. You need both. Non-negotiable.
TypeWhat it doesWhen to applyProductsCritical note
Pre-emergent 🛡️Soil barrier stops seeds germinatingFeb–Mar (55°F) & Aug–Sep (below 70°F)Prodiamine, Scotts Halts, DithiopyrKILLS GRASS SEED TOO. No seeding within 6–8 weeks.
Post-emergent selective 🎯Kills specific weeds, not grassWhen weeds visible. Wait 2–3 days after mowing.Ortho Weed B Gon, Spectracide Weed StopUnder 85°F. No rain in 24hrs. Calm winds only.
Non-selective ☢️Kills EVERYTHINGBeds, paths, borders only. Not on lawn.Roundup, generic glyphosateWait 2 weeks before replanting. Seriously.
Weed & Feed 💊Post-emergent + fertilizer comboSpring or fall when weeds actively growingScotts Turf Builder W+F3–4 weeks gap from pre-emergent application.
CrabgrassHIGH
Annual summer weed. Germinates at 55°F. Dense and spreading. Nearly impossible to kill once established. The crabgrass does not negotiate.
Pre-emergent Feb–Mar. Do not miss the window.
DandelionMED
Deep taproot. Regrows from root fragments. Spreads by parachute. Looks cheerful. Is not your friend.
Broadleaf post-emergent anytime visible.
Oxalis / CloverHIGH
Spreads aggressively. Very common in Sacramento clay. Multiple treatments required. Will outlast your patience if you let it.
Selective broadleaf. Expect 2–3 treatments.
Chickweed / FilareeMED
Winter annuals. Germinate in fall, appear in spring. Block with fall pre-emergent in August. Stop them before they exist.
Fall pre-emergent Aug–Sep is the key move.
BindweedHIGH
The nightmare. Deep roots. Vines everything. Has survived dinosaurs, probably. Requires months of glyphosate persistence.
Glyphosate on vines in beds only. Persistence required.
SpurgeLOW
Low-growing summer annual. Loves hot dry soil — practically built for Citrus Heights summers. Spring pre-emergent handles it well.
Spring pre-emergent is sufficient.
